## **Anne Sergerie**

**Role:** Customer Service Manager / Customer Service Representative Lead

### Primary Responsibilities:

- Customer service operations and team management
- Order management and customer account oversight
- Customer onboarding and relationship management
- Claims and credit management
- Service level monitoring

### Key Pain Points Reported:

1. **Customer Data Visibility** - Cannot view customer profiles (addresses, incoterms, pricing, payment terms) without creating an order
2. **Commercial Agreements** - No visibility on commercial agreements and rebate structures; difficult to see overall customer situation
3. **Claims Management** - Quality claims currently managed via Monday.com; no SAP claim numbers issued to customers
4. **Service KPIs** - Wants daily dashboard showing:
   - Orders not delivered on time (with causes)
   - Delivery performance metrics
   - Customer service evaluation KPIs
5. **Inventory Adjustments** - Unclear process for adjusting inventory after stock refusals or wrong product deliveries
6. **Photo Access** - CSR team doesn't have access to loading photos needed for claim investigations

### Challenges:

- Difficulty being proactive without proper visibility
- Hard to manage and motivate team without performance data
- Back-and-forth between departments for information
- Late submission of claim disputes to customers

### Process Areas Involved:

- Customer Experience Design
- Customer Account Management
- Sales Orders Management
- Warranty/Claims Processing
- Service Quality Evaluation

### Primary Responsibilities:

- Production planning and scheduling
- Demand forecasting and forecast accuracy
- Materials planning (especially bentonite)
- Production capacity management
- Product lifecycle transitions

   - File not being updated

   - Lack of stakeholder alignment

3. **Production Prioritization** - Production plan treats all orders equally (stock building vs urgent customer orders)

   - Lack of visibility causes poor decisions

- Inventory management in plants not reliable
- Procedures from June 2024 unclear/outdated

- Risk of producing/ordering wrong items
- Multiple stakeholders, no orchestrator

**Finance:**

9. **Invoice Visibility** - Cannot see previously approved invoices (risk of duplicate approvals)

## **Nicolas**

**Role:** Logistics Manager / Operations Manager

### Primary Responsibilities:

- Logistics operations across all plants
- Transportation management (domestic and international)
- Warehouse operations and inventory management
- Export documentation and customs
- Freight booking and carrier management
- Operations reporting and analysis

### Key Pain Points Reported:

**Reporting & Visibility:**

1. **Excel Report Automation** - Critical reports require daily manual updates:
   - "SAP Order Status Logistics" (used by all departments)
   - "Capacity Check" (used by all plants + planning)
   - "KPI Ops"
   - Information not updated during the day
2. **Pallet Reporting** - Reports show units instead of pallets; requires manual conversion
3. **Transport Rate Visibility** - No tracking of average transport rates by ship-to vs budget
4. **Monthly GL Reports** - No monthly report by GL and cost center; "managing in the dark"

**Transportation Management:**

5. **Commercial Invoices F8** - Logistics employees need access to generate these 6. **Export Documents** - Manual creation of Word/Excel export documents (e.g., plastic weight forms)

- Information exists in SAP but must be retyped

7. **Transport Rate Validation** - No tool to validate rates quarterly
   - Rates valid "forever" in SAP
   - Risk of incorrect accruals
   - Plants may not challenge rates or find cheaper carriers
8. **Freight Orders** - Can't use "subcontracting" option
   - Manual emails to carriers
   - No confirmation visibility
9. **Ocean Freight Updates** - Need mass update capability for booking changes (vessel, dates)
   - Changes almost daily
   - Must update each booking individually

**Inventory Management:**

10. **Inventory Team** - Need dedicated "operations" team for inventory processes and analysis - Lack of standardization between plants - Finished goods inventory inaccurate - Shortage issues (short-shipping) - Raw material inventory issues
11. **Inventory Process Documentation** - Need written processes for inventory movements per plant
12. **Pallet Scanning Issue** - Can scan same pallet twice at production exit, inflating stock 13. **Return Process** - Unclear process when customer refuses a pallet
13. **Cycle Counting** - Not implemented properly in Canada - St-Alexis and Etobicoke do 3-4/week autonomously - Yanik started in Boucherville, Newton, Notre-Dame
14. **Cycle Count Analysis** - Results not published or analyzed

**Process & Standardization:**

16. **SAP Process Documentation** - Need written SAP processes
17. **Plant Information Access** - Plants lack centralized product info (pallet patterns)
18. **Accruals Management** - Need bi-weekly/monthly meeting to resolve accrual errors

**Procurement:**

19. **One-off Suppliers** - No process for handling occasional suppliers (small invoices)

### Challenges:

- Managing operations across multiple plants without standardization
- Critical decisions made without proper data/visibility
- High manual effort for routine tasks
- Inventory accuracy issues causing operational problems
- Lack of process documentation and training materials

### Process Areas Involved:

- Order-to-Cash
- Forecast-to-Stock
- Logistics and Warehousing
- Outbound Transportation
- Customs and Global Trade
- Inventory Management
- Cycle Counting
- Business Process Management
- Procure-to-Pay

## **Julien Chenard**

**Role:** Procurement Manager / Buyer / Supply Chain Manager

### Primary Responsibilities:

- Raw material procurement
- Supplier management and quality
- Purchase order creation and management
- Inventory strategy
- Materials planning
- Project management

### Key Pain Points Reported:

**Supplier Quality:**

1. **Raw Material Non-Conformities** - Process unclear
   - Non-conformities don't reach suppliers (or arrive late)
   - Poor tracking of supplier credits
   - Can't see supplier performance over time
   - No performance indicators

**Inventory Management:**

2. **Inventory Ownership** - Unclear who is responsible for inventory

- Who analyzes inventory variances?
- Who decides to scrap bags?
- No clear owner
- No inventory strategy (don't want sleeping stock, seasonality management)

3. **Inventory Imprecision** - SAP inventory not accurate
   - Impact: Backorders (can't serve customers), excess stock (cash flow issues)
4. **Consumables Visibility** - No visibility on non-SAP consumables
   - Airbags, supersacks at ICE
   - Don't know when to order
   - Risk of running out
   - Currently: individual asks plant instead of automated flag

**SAP Support:**

5. **SAP Troubleshooting** - Lack of competent resources to troubleshoot SAP problems

**Procurement Processes:**

6. **Material Requests** - Non-uniform process for limestone, boxes, pallets across plants

- Email, Teams, various methods
- Need uniform process (possibly requisitions)

7. **Limestone Freight Orders** - Managing multiple FOs per day at Boucherville and Windsor
   - Many manipulations, time waste, error risk
   - IT interventions needed

**Planning & Visibility:**

8. **Project Resource Management** - Unclear resource capacity for projects

- Who has time?
- Are delivery dates realistic?
- People forced to choose between projects and day-to-day

9. **Production Location Changes** - No process for changing where a product is made
   - Is material available at new location?
   - Can supplier deliver there?
   - Risk of missing raw materials
10. **PO Creation Triggers** - Need to map what triggers PO creation
    - Currently uses "Supply & Demand" file (designed by Eric)
11. **Long-term Demand Visibility** - No visibility on long-term raw material demand
    - Currently using Excel "Supply and Demand" file
    - Issue with long lead time materials
12. **Order Promising** - Sometimes promise delivery dates without checking raw material availability

**Forecasting:**

13. **Sales Forecast Accuracy** - Forecasts not precise enough - Seen cases where orders were 5x forecast level - Impact: Stock shortages, customer dissatisfaction, expensive air freight, panic mode

### Challenges:

- Managing procurement without adequate visibility tools
- Lack of standardized processes
- Knowledge in individuals' heads, not documented
- Inventory ownership ambiguity
- Balancing strategic projects with operational demands
- High lead time materials require better long-term visibility

### Process Areas Involved:

- Procure-to-Pay
- Supplier Quality Management
- Supplier Recovery
- Inventory Management (raw materials and finished goods)
- Forecast-to-Stock
- Plan-to-Produce
- Demand Management
- Project Management
- Business Capability Development
